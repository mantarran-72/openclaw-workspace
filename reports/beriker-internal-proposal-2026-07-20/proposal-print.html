Internal Recommendation

  • Do not bid this like a simple 111 sq ft room addition. The risk is in matching existing conditions, fire separation, slab/foundation interface, exterior envelope details, finish tie-ins, inspections, and owner selections.
  • Aggressive floor: $138,000-$145,000 only if the site visit confirms clean existing conditions and simple owner selections.
  • Safer carried range: $155,000-$175,000.
  • High-risk range: $185,000+ if slab/foundation, electrical, HVAC, doors/windows, or finish matching expands.

Key Internal Risk Notes

  • Field verify dimensions before construction. A3 explicitly calls this out.
  • Existing slab/foundation condition is a major risk. If the slab cannot remain, issue change order.
  • Fire separation is not ordinary drywall: include required gypsum board and fire caulking between carport and living space.
  • Exact match of aged siding, trim, flooring, cabinetry, paint, and texture should be excluded unless verified.
  • Electrical service/panel upgrades and HVAC redesign are excluded unless confirmed and added.
  • Owner-selected windows, doors, refrigerator, hardware, flooring, and finish materials must stay within allowance or trigger adjustment.

Suggested Client-Facing Scope

Pacific Houseworks proposes to convert approximately 111 sq ft of the existing carport into conditioned dining space per the approved plans, including selective demolition, foundation/raised floor work, exterior wall framing, weatherproofing, siding/trim tie-ins, new doors/windows, fire separation between the remaining carport and living space, electrical/HVAC coordination, refrigerator relocation access, cabinet/end panel tie-ins, insulation, drywall, interior trim, flooring/paint allowances, inspection coordination, and final cleanup.
