## Internal Recommendation

Recommended carried client-facing base price: **$159,800**

Pricing posture:

- **Do not bid this like a simple 111 sq ft room addition.** It is a small footprint, but the risk is in matching existing conditions, carport-to-living-space fire separation, slab/foundation interface, exterior envelope details, finish tie-ins, inspections, and owner-selected doors/windows/cabinet finishes.
- **Floor to consider if Ethan wants to be aggressive:** $138,000-$145,000, only if site visit confirms clean slab/foundation conditions, owner supplies/locks selections early, and no panel/HVAC complications.
- **Safer carried range:** $155,000-$175,000.
- **High-risk range:** $185,000+ if slab/foundation must be reworked, exterior matching is fussy, doors/windows are premium/long-lead, or electrical/HVAC upgrades are required.

This internal estimate uses a blended production labor rate of **$85/hr** before overhead/profit/contingency. It is not yet a fixed-price contract.

## Phase Scope Notes

### 1. Preconstruction

Included:

- Site verification of existing dimensions, slab elevations, beam/post conditions, carport drainage, existing electrical/HVAC access, and finish tie-in conditions.
- Review of plan set and owner selections.
- Coordination of inspection sequence.
- Clarification log for any conflict between existing conditions and drawings.

Internal risk note: A3 specifically calls for field verification of existing measurements and dimensions before construction. Do not lock a fixed price until this is done.

### 2. Mobilization And Protection

Included:

- Work area setup.
- Protection of occupied home, porch/carport approach, existing finishes, and landscaping immediately affected by access.
- Dust control and temporary separation at kitchen/living-side openings.
- Daily cleanup appropriate for an occupied residence.

### 3. Selective Demolition

Included:

- Remove/demolish only the existing walls, siding/trim, doors, plumbing fixtures/casework, and other items shown as removed/demo on A3.
- Keep existing carport slab where acceptable.
- Preserve remaining carport, existing roof structure, existing posts/beam where plans require.
- Debris loading, dump fees, and normal disposal.

Not included:

- Hazardous materials testing/remediation.
- Major structural demolition beyond the shown conversion area.

### 4. Foundation / Raised Floor / Slab Interface

Plan basis:

- A5 foundation plan.
- A5.1 foundation details.
- Existing concrete slab can remain in several details, but plan requires verification and proper vapor/raised floor treatment.
- Special inspection is required at epoxy dowels per foundation detail notes.

Included:

- Field verification of existing slab/foundation.
- Layout and construct raised floor/foundation interface per plan details.
- Anchor bolts, epoxy dowels, vapor barrier/moisture control, blocking, venting as required.
- Maintain slope/drainage away from new foundation and exterior slabs.
- Coordinate special inspection where required.

Internal risk note: This is one of the biggest uncertainty items. If the existing slab is unsuitable, out of level, cracked, missing proper base, or conflicts with floor elevation, carry a change order.

### 5. Exterior Framing / Building Shell

Plan basis:

- A3 proposed floor plan.
- A6 roof plan.
- A7 building sections.
- D1/D2 flashing, siding, and framing details.

Included:

- New exterior wall framing under existing continuous carport beam.
- Preserve existing roof/beam/posts where plans show to remain.
- Sheathing, weather-resistive barrier, flashing, sealants, blocking, siding, trim, and tie-ins.
- Match existing board/batten or siding profile as practically available.
- Carport-facing fire separation build-up where required.

Internal risk note: Exact match of aged siding/trim/paint should be excluded from fixed price unless verified and selected.

### 6. Windows / Exterior Doors / Glazing

Plan basis:

- A3 floor plan key note 9.
- D1 door/window flashing details.
- Title 24 glazing requirements.

Included allowance:

- New exterior French doors and windows by Marvin, Milgard, or equal.
- Black frames/grids to match existing where selected.
- Manufacturer sill pans or field-fabricated metal sill pan details.
- Flashing and casing to match existing as practical.

Internal risk note: $14,500 material allowance is a placeholder. Final cost depends heavily on manufacturer, grille pattern, lead time, door size, and hardware selection.

### 7. Electrical

Plan basis:

- A4 electrical plan.
- High-efficiency lighting and California lighting requirements.
- Smoke/CO and GFCI/weatherproof requirements shown in the notes.

Included:

- New dining room receptacles and lighting per plan/code.
- Switches/dimmers where shown.
- Exterior/weatherproof/GFCI items where affected.
- Smoke/CO updates where required by inspector.
- Standard finish devices and plates.

Excluded unless confirmed:

- Service upgrade.
- Panel replacement.
- Major circuit rerouting due to inaccessible existing conditions.
- Smart-home/low-voltage beyond shown requirements.

### 8. HVAC / Appliance Rough-In

Plan basis:

- A4 shows toe-kick heat register / HVAC coordination notes.
- A3 requires refrigerator/freezer relocation/access into new dining room.

Included:

- Basic register extension or relocation allowance.
- Refrigerator location coordination.
- Water/ice maker rough-in allowance if needed near final refrigerator location.
- Minor patching from affected routes.

Internal risk note: HVAC load/design adequacy is not fully proven from the drawings. If the existing system cannot serve the new conditioned room or duct routing is blocked, carry a change order.

### 9. Cabinet / Kitchen Tie-In

Plan basis:

- A3 key note 6: new refrigerator cabinet and end cabinet face to match existing.
- Plan notes call for paint-grade kitchen cabinets as required for new work to match existing.

Included:

- Refrigerator cabinet/end panel modifications.
- Paint-grade panel/cabinet allowance.
- Finish carpentry tie-ins around new opening/access.
- Coordination with owner on countertop/back splash impact.

Excluded unless added:

- Full kitchen cabinet replacement.
- Full countertop replacement.
- Appliance package beyond listed refrigerator/freezer allowance.

### 10. Insulation / Drywall / Fire Separation

Plan basis:

- A3 key note 7.
- A7 building section notes.
- D1 fire separation detail.

Included:

- Living-space wall insulation, attic/ceiling/floor insulation per plan/code.
- Fire separation between carport and living space due to attic space above.
- 1/2 in gypsum board at adjacent carport walls/ceiling as noted, with required fire caulking.
- Drywall hang/tape/texture at new dining area and disturbed tie-ins.

Internal risk note: Fire separation needs careful execution and inspection documentation. Do not treat this as ordinary drywall only.

### 11. Interior Finishes

Included allowance:

- Interior trim/casing/base to match existing as practical.
- Paint and texture matching at new/damaged surfaces.
- Flooring allowance for new dining room and transition areas.
- Finish hardware allowance.
- Finish material samples/approval coordination.

Internal risk note: Finish selections can swing this phase fast. The plan itself says bidders should set adequate cost allowances and submit samples with bid.

### 12. Exterior Concrete / Drainage / Weatherproofing

Included:

- Exterior landing/slab touch-up where directly affected by new door/opening work.
- Slope adjustments immediately adjacent to the new foundation/slab interface where practical.
- Flashing/weatherproofing closeout at exterior wall bases and openings.

Excluded unless verified and added:

- Broad driveway/carport slab replacement.
- Drainage redesign.
- Landscape/irrigation repair beyond directly disturbed work.

### 13. Closeout

Included:

- Final cleanup.
- Punch list.
- Inspection closeout coordination.
- Photo documentation of concealed fire separation/flashing/foundation conditions where useful.

## Exclusions To Preserve Margin

- Permit fees, plan revision fees, design/engineering fees, and agency fees.
- Special inspection fees above allowance.
- Hazardous materials testing/remediation, asbestos, lead, mold, rot, termite, or hidden damage.
- Full electrical panel/service upgrade.
- Major HVAC redesign or equipment replacement.
- Major plumbing reroutes outside the directly affected area.
- Full kitchen remodel, full countertop replacement, or full cabinet replacement.
- Exact match of aged/discontinued siding, trim, flooring, cabinetry, paint, stain, hardware, or textures.
- Broad concrete slab replacement unless specifically added.
- Landscaping, irrigation, hardscape, roofing, gutters, or drainage redesign beyond the directly affected work.
- Owner-caused delay, late selections, special-order lead times, and defective/incomplete owner-furnished items.

## Change Order Triggers

Use these as hard internal guardrails:

- Existing carport slab cannot remain or cannot support planned raised floor/foundation assembly.
- Existing posts/beam conditions differ from plans.
- Existing floor elevations do not align with proposed dining room without extra framing/leveling.
- Inspector requires additional fire separation, special inspection, smoke/CO updates, or CalGreen corrections.
- Existing panel capacity or circuit routing is inadequate.
- HVAC cannot be extended simply to the dining room.
- Owner selects premium doors/windows, unusual grille pattern, upgraded hardware, or long-lead products above allowance.
- Cabinet/countertop/backsplash scope grows beyond refrigerator-access tie-in.
- Hidden water damage, dry rot, termites, mold, lead/asbestos, or nonconforming previous work is discovered.

## Internal Bottom Line

This should be treated as a **$160k small addition/conversion**, not a $50k handyman job. The footprint is small, but the project carries real GC risk: permitted conditioned-space conversion, fire separation, exterior envelope, foundation/slab uncertainty, finish matching, and owner selections.

If Ethan wants to win the work aggressively, the lowest defensible move is probably around **$145k with tight allowances and explicit exclusions**. Below that, the job can still be profitable only if the slab/foundation/electrical/HVAC conditions are unusually clean and the owner keeps selections simple.
