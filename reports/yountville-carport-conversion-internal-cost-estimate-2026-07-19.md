## Estimate Position

This is an internal job-cost estimate for converting part of the existing carport into conditioned dining space, with affected kitchen/refrigerator work, owner-furnished doors/windows, new exterior envelope work, raised floor/foundation work, finish tie-ins, inspections, and required trade coordination.

This is not client-facing contract language. Use it to set a proposal target, define allowances, and protect against field-condition risk.

## Major Assumptions

- Converted conditioned area is 110 sf per Ethan's plan readback / project clarification.
- Owner supplies exterior doors, windows, glazing units, and manufacturer hardware unless separately added.
- Contractor installs owner-furnished doors/windows only.
- Existing roof, rafters, main carport beams/posts, AC condenser, water heater, EV charger, and remaining carport stay in place unless directly disturbed.
- No electrical service upgrade included.
- HVAC assumes duct/register/return accommodation only, not full equipment replacement.
- Refrigerator area includes cabinet/ref panel allowance and minor electrical/plumbing coordination only.
- Interior finish level assumes clean premium small-project execution, not luxury custom millwork throughout the existing kitchen.
- Permit fees, designer/engineer fees, utility fees, special inspection, soils/compaction testing, and agency fees are excluded unless added.
- Hidden rot, termite damage, mold, asbestos, lead paint, concealed structural defects, drainage failure, or nonconforming existing work are change-order conditions.

## Overhead, Profit, and Proposal Target

For this job, a normal low-margin handyman markup is the wrong pricing model. This is a permitted small addition in a high-cost location with owner-furnished product risk, finish matching, inspections, scheduling burden, and concealed-condition exposure.

| Pricing Layer | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Internal cost with contingency | $71,038 |
| Overhead/profit at 25% | $17,760 |
| **Recommended base proposal target** | **$89,000** |

Recommended client-facing structure:

- Base proposal target: $88,000-$95,000.
- Do not go below: $78,000 unless scope is materially reduced and verified.
- Strong ask: $102,000-$115,000 if schedule compression, premium finish matching, or broad cabinet scope is expected.
- Internal comfort zone after field verification: direct costs need to land below roughly $65,000 for an $88,000 proposal to feel controlled.

## Suggested Bid Presentation

Use a fixed-price proposal only after a paid site-verification step or after the following are confirmed:

- Approved permit set and revision date.
- Existing carport dimensions, slab/grade, beam/post bearing, and finish-floor elevation.
- Door/window rough openings, labels, sizes, handing, and delivery dates.
- Electrical panel capacity and route.
- HVAC supply/return requirement.
- Refrigerator appliance specs and cabinet requirements.
- Flooring, siding, trim, paint, ceiling finish, and fixture selections.

Recommended client-facing framing:

> Preliminary budget for the permitted 110 sf carport-to-dining conversion, based on the current plan set and owner-furnished doors/windows, is expected to fall around the high-$80k to mid-$90k range depending on final field verification and finish selections. A fixed contract price should follow site verification, owner-furnished product review, and allowance confirmation.

Keep the detailed labor/material/subcontractor breakdown internal. Give the client a clean base price, allowance schedule, exclusions, alternates, and change-order terms.

## High-Risk Change-Order Items

- Existing framing, beams, posts, slab, or drainage differ from the drawings.
- Door/window units are late, damaged, mis-sized, missing hardware, missing Title 24 labels, or not code compliant.
- Electrical panel cannot accept required circuits/protection.
- HVAC load/ducting/return-air requirement is more involved than plan assumptions.
- Cabinet match requires custom shop work or broader kitchen finish work.
- Existing hidden damage appears after selective demolition.
- Inspector requires additional fire separation, hardware, structural corrections, or documentation.

## Pricing Verdict

Recommended internal target:

- Direct cost before contingency: about $65k.
- Internal cost with contingency: about $71k.

- Reasonable proposal range: $88k-$95k base, with alternates and exclusions.

This is a premium small GC project. Price it like controlled, permitted residential construction in Yountville, not a handyman enclosure.
